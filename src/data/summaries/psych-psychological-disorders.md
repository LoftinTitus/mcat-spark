# Psychological Disorders

## Overview
Psychological disorders, also known as mental disorders, are patterns of thoughts, feelings, or behaviors that cause significant distress or impairment in functioning. The MCAT emphasizes the classification, symptoms, causes, and treatment of major psychological disorders, as well as their impact on individuals and society.

## Classification and Diagnosis
- **DSM-5:** The Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders, Fifth Edition, is the standard classification system in the U.S.
- **Criteria:** Disorders are diagnosed based on specific symptom patterns, duration, and impact on functioning.
- **Prevalence:** Psychological disorders are common and can affect anyone, regardless of age, gender, or background.

## Major Categories of Psychological Disorders

### 1. Anxiety Disorders
- **Examples:** Generalized anxiety disorder, panic disorder, phobias, social anxiety disorder
- **Symptoms:** Excessive fear, worry, avoidance, physical symptoms (e.g., palpitations, sweating)

### 2. Mood Disorders
- **Examples:** Major depressive disorder, bipolar disorder
- **Symptoms:** Persistent sadness, loss of interest, changes in sleep/appetite, mania (bipolar)

### 3. Schizophrenia Spectrum and Other Psychotic Disorders
- **Symptoms:** Delusions, hallucinations, disorganized thinking, negative symptoms (e.g., flat affect)

### 4. Trauma- and Stressor-Related Disorders
- **Examples:** Post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD), acute stress disorder
- **Symptoms:** Intrusive memories, avoidance, hyperarousal, negative mood

### 5. Obsessive-Compulsive and Related Disorders
- **Examples:** Obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD), body dysmorphic disorder
- **Symptoms:** Obsessions (intrusive thoughts), compulsions (repetitive behaviors)

### 6. Somatic Symptom and Related Disorders
- **Examples:** Somatic symptom disorder, illness anxiety disorder, conversion disorder
- **Symptoms:** Physical symptoms without medical explanation, excessive health anxiety

### 7. Dissociative Disorders
- **Examples:** Dissociative identity disorder, dissociative amnesia
- **Symptoms:** Disruptions in memory, identity, or consciousness

### 8. Personality Disorders
- **Examples:** Borderline, antisocial, narcissistic, avoidant personality disorders
- **Symptoms:** Enduring patterns of maladaptive behavior, cognition, and inner experience

### 9. Neurodevelopmental and Neurocognitive Disorders
- **Examples:** Autism spectrum disorder, ADHD, Alzheimerâ€™s disease, delirium
- **Symptoms:** Impairments in cognition, memory, attention, or social functioning

## Causes and Risk Factors
- **Biological:** Genetics, neurotransmitter imbalances, brain structure/function
- **Psychological:** Trauma, stress, maladaptive thought patterns
- **Social:** Family, culture, socioeconomic status, stigma

## Treatment and Prevention
- **Psychotherapy:** Cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT), psychodynamic therapy, group therapy
- **Pharmacotherapy:** Antidepressants, antipsychotics, anxiolytics, mood stabilizers
- **Community and Social Support:** Education, support groups, reducing stigma

## MCAT Connections
- **Application:** Recognize symptoms, risk factors, and treatments in clinical vignettes.
- **Research:** Understand prevalence, comorbidity, and the biopsychosocial model.
- **Critical Thinking:** Consider ethical, cultural, and social implications of diagnosis and treatment.

**Q2: Which of the following is a positive symptom of schizophrenia?**
A) Flat affect
B) Delusions *[CORRECT]
C) Social withdrawal
D) Lack of motivation
**Explanation:** Positive symptoms add abnormal experiences, such as delusions and hallucinations.

**Q3: What is a key feature of obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD)?**
A) Dissociation
B) Intrusive thoughts and repetitive behaviors *[CORRECT]
C) Mood swings
D) Memory loss
**Explanation:** OCD is characterized by obsessions (intrusive thoughts) and compulsions (repetitive behaviors).

**Q4: Which therapy focuses on changing maladaptive thought patterns and behaviors?**
A) Psychodynamic therapy
B) Cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT) *[CORRECT]
C) Group therapy
D) Pharmacotherapy
**Explanation:** CBT is an evidence-based therapy that targets dysfunctional thinking and behavior.

**Q5: Which of the following is a neurodevelopmental disorder?**
