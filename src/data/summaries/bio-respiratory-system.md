## Respiratory System: Structure, Function, and MCAT Relevance

### Overview
The respiratory system enables gas exchange between the atmosphere and the bloodstream, supplying oxygen for cellular metabolism and removing carbon dioxide, a metabolic waste. It also plays roles in pH regulation, vocalization, and immune defense.

### Anatomy of the Respiratory System
- **Upper respiratory tract:**
  - **Nasal cavity:** Warms, humidifies, and filters air; olfaction
  - **Pharynx:** Passageway for air and food
  - **Larynx:** Voice box; contains vocal cords; protects lower airway
- **Lower respiratory tract:**
  - **Trachea:** Windpipe; supported by cartilage rings
  - **Bronchi:** Branches into left/right lungs; further divides into bronchioles
  - **Bronchioles:** Smallest airways; smooth muscle controls airflow
  - **Alveoli:** Tiny sacs for gas exchange; surrounded by capillaries

### Alveolar Gas Exchange
- **Type I alveolar cells:** Form alveolar wall; site of gas exchange
- **Type II alveolar cells:** Secrete surfactant (reduces surface tension, prevents collapse)
- **Respiratory membrane:** Thin barrier (alveolar epithelium, capillary endothelium, basement membrane) for efficient diffusion

### Mechanics of Breathing
- **Inspiration:** Diaphragm contracts (flattens), external intercostals lift ribcage; thoracic volume increases, pressure decreases, air flows in
- **Expiration:** Diaphragm relaxes, internal intercostals contract (forced expiration); thoracic volume decreases, pressure increases, air flows out
- **Control:** Medulla oblongata (respiratory center) regulates rate/depth; chemoreceptors sense CO₂, O₂, pH

### Gas Transport
- **Oxygen:** Transported mainly bound to hemoglobin (Hb) in red blood cells
- **Carbon dioxide:** Transported as bicarbonate (HCO₃⁻, via carbonic anhydrase), dissolved in plasma, or bound to Hb
- **Oxyhemoglobin dissociation curve:** Sigmoidal; affected by pH (Bohr effect), CO₂, temperature, 2,3-BPG

### Regulation of Blood pH
- **CO₂ + H₂O ↔ H₂CO₃ ↔ H⁺ + HCO₃⁻** (carbonic acid-bicarbonate buffer)
- **Hyperventilation:** ↓ CO₂, ↑ pH (respiratory alkalosis)
- **Hypoventilation:** ↑ CO₂, ↓ pH (respiratory acidosis)

### MCAT Clinical Relevance
- **Asthma:** Bronchoconstriction, airway inflammation
- **COPD:** Chronic bronchitis/emphysema; impaired airflow/gas exchange
- **Pulmonary edema:** Fluid in alveoli impairs gas exchange
- **Surfactant deficiency:** Neonatal respiratory distress syndrome
- **Carbon monoxide poisoning:** CO binds Hb, blocks O₂ transport
- **Acid-base disorders:** Respiratory acidosis/alkalosis

## Quiz

**Q1: What is the primary site of gas exchange in the lungs?**
A) Bronchi
B) Alveoli *[CORRECT]
C) Trachea
D) Pharynx
**Explanation:** Alveoli are tiny sacs where oxygen and carbon dioxide are exchanged with blood.

**Q2: Which cell type produces surfactant in the alveoli?**
A) Type I alveolar cell
B) Type II alveolar cell *[CORRECT]
C) Macrophage
D) Goblet cell
**Explanation:** Type II alveolar cells secrete surfactant, reducing surface tension and preventing alveolar collapse.

**Q3: What effect does hyperventilation have on blood pH?**
A) Decreases pH (acidosis)
B) Increases pH (alkalosis) *[CORRECT]
C) No effect
D) Causes metabolic acidosis
**Explanation:** Hyperventilation lowers CO₂, raising blood pH (respiratory alkalosis).

**Q4: Which structure controls the rate and depth of breathing?**
A) Cerebellum
B) Medulla oblongata *[CORRECT]
C) Hypothalamus
D) Thalamus
**Explanation:** The medulla oblongata contains the respiratory centers that regulate breathing.

**Q5: What is the main way CO₂ is transported in the blood?**
A) Dissolved in plasma
B) Bound to hemoglobin
C) As bicarbonate ion (HCO₃⁻) *[CORRECT]
D) As carbonic acid
**Explanation:** Most CO₂ is converted to bicarbonate ion for transport in the blood.
